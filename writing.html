i. Mikhail M. Bakhtin (Михаил Михайлович Бахтин)

Mikhail M. Bakhtin was a Russian philosopher. He is best known for his works on Rabelais and Dostoevsky, the concept of the chronotope in the novel, and speech-genres in communication. Bakhtin’s concepts often outshine his biography, despite the crucial role Bakhtin’s lived experience playing in his writings. For my master’s thesis, I wrote a glossary which included eleven of Bakhtin’s concepts, and placed these concepts within the context of Bakhtin’s writings and Bakhtin’s reality of Stalinist Russia. After finishing my thesis, I am still committed to working with Bakhtin and his writings. For the past year I have been learning Russian, in order to read Bakhtin in his own language. ii. Poetry

In addition to my interests in Bakhtin and Russian literature, I enjoy writing poems. Here is a poem that I wrote during a trip to Corsica:

BONIFACIO

Ants on the windshield. You ask: how
do we know we are where, without
a trumpet directing us past cows
and bulls? Do you think cliffs whip clouds

into tiramisu, and that ants play organs?
Traffic marmalade: this one road
allergic to a traffic light, causing boredom
between cacti. And midnight toads

are a punk band under the stars
belching nonsense at your parents.
We are between the sea and far
from the cliffs on Aragon’s steps,

but we maze to the cemetery in anonymity:
si git une inconnue. Our organs in proximity.



iii. The Honorable Murph

The Honorable Murph is a novel about a disgraced judge who is transferred to The Pushkin,
a prison ship floating among the Great Lakes. The novel follows Tully Murphy, as he makes
his way from the floor of his chambers to The Pushkin's lower decks.

The Honorable Murph is a work in progress.

J. G. Sheppard - 2020
